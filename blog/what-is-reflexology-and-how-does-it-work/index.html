What Is Reflexology and How Does It Work?

03 June 2026

What Is Reflexology?

Reflexology is a complementary therapy that involves applying gentle pressure to specific points on the feet, hands, or ears. These points are believed to correspond with different areas of the body.

Many people seek reflexology to support relaxation, reduce stress, and encourage a sense of overall wellbeing.

How Does Reflexology Work?

According to reflexology theory, different areas of the feet correspond to different organs and systems throughout the body.

By applying pressure to these areas, reflexologists aim to encourage balance and support the body's natural processes.

While reflexology should not be considered a replacement for medical treatment, many clients find it deeply relaxing and restorative.

What Happens During a Session?

A typical session begins with a brief discussion about your health, wellbeing, and goals.

You will usually remain fully clothed while relaxing in a comfortable position as pressure is applied to specific reflex points.

Many people describe the experience as calming and grounding.

Potential Benefits of Reflexology

People often choose reflexology to support:

  • Stress management
  • Relaxation
  • Better sleep
  • General wellbeing
  • Mind-body awareness

Individual experiences vary, and reflexology is best viewed as part of a broader approach to self-care and wellbeing.

Is Reflexology Right for You?

If you are looking for a gentle, non-invasive therapy that promotes relaxation and wellbeing, reflexology may be worth exploring.

If you have questions about whether reflexology is suitable for your circumstances, it is always a good idea to discuss them before booking.
