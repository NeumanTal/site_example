What to Expect During Your First Reflexology Session

If you've never experienced reflexology before, it's natural to have questions about what happens during a session.

Understanding the process can help you feel more comfortable and confident before your appointment.

Before Your Appointment

Your first session will usually begin with a short conversation about your general wellbeing, lifestyle, and any concerns you would like to discuss.

This is an opportunity to ask questions and ensure that the treatment is tailored to your needs.

What Should I Wear?

Reflexology is typically performed on the feet, so comfortable clothing is recommended.

You will remain fully clothed throughout the session, with only your feet needing to be accessible for treatment.

During the Treatment

You will be invited to sit or lie down in a comfortable position while pressure is applied to specific areas of the feet.

The pressure is usually gentle and adjusted according to your comfort level.

Many people find the experience deeply relaxing and some even fall asleep during treatment.

Does Reflexology Hurt?

Reflexology should not be painful.

Certain areas of the feet may feel more sensitive than others, but treatment should always remain within a comfortable range.

Communication is encouraged throughout the session so adjustments can be made whenever necessary.

How Long Does a Session Last?

Most reflexology sessions last between 45 and 60 minutes.

Your therapist will explain the structure of the session and answer any questions before treatment begins.

What Might I Feel Afterwards?

Many people report feeling:

  • Relaxed
  • Refreshed
  • Calm
  • More connected to their body

Experiences vary from person to person, and there is no single "correct" response to treatment.

How Many Sessions Do I Need?

The number of sessions depends on your goals and personal preferences.

Some people enjoy occasional treatments for relaxation, while others choose to book regular sessions as part of their self-care routine.

Preparing for Your First Session

To help you get the most from your appointment:

  • Arrive a few minutes early
  • Wear comfortable clothing
  • Stay hydrated
  • Allow yourself time to relax afterwards if possible
